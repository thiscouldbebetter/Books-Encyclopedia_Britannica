BARSI, a town of British India, in the Sholapur district of Bombay, lying
within a tract entirely surrounded by the Nizam's dominions. Pop. (1901)
24,242. Barsi is a flourishing centre of trade, exporting to Bombay large
quantities of cotton and oil-seeds. It has several factories for ginning
and pressing cotton--some on a large scale. It is connected with the main
line of the Great Indian Peninsula railway by a light railway.
